# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Local Boise, Idaho customers — mostly discovering Mckenna through Instagram (@mckennas.baked.goods) or word of mouth, arriving **on their phone**. They're planning a birthday, baby/bridal shower, or small gathering and want a custom cake or bake box, or they just want a small-batch treat for a brunch table or everyday. Their mental question is "is her stuff good, and how do I order?" — they're evaluating craft and figuring out the next step in one short visit.

## Product Purpose

The polished front door for a home-based, small-batch bakery. It exists to convert Instagram-level interest into **custom-order inquiries** that land in the bakery's inbox (mckennasbakedgoods@gmail.com). It showcases her bakes and her story — Le Cordon Bleu London trained, French pâtisserie technique fused with American home-style — to build trust fast. Success = a visitor goes from "saw a photo on IG" to "submitted an order inquiry" without friction.

## Brand Personality

Warm, homey, and modern with a personal, hand-made charm. Aspirational but approachable — the curated-but-cozy "Instagram/Pinterest lifestyle" feeling, not a luxury maison. Voice is friendly and first-person ("I bake…"), inviting, a little sweet, never corporate and never childish. Three words: **warm, crafted, inviting.**

## Anti-references

- Sterile, cold high-end French patisserie *brand* sites (Ladurée-style minimalism) — explicitly NOT this.
- Generic Squarespace/Wix stock-bakery templates; the cream-background + gold-serif "AI bakery" cliché.
- Childish cupcake-clipart cutesy (kawaii pastel overload, comic-sans energy).
- Cluttered, loud, everything-everywhere layouts.

## Design Principles

1. **Let the bakes be the hero.** Photography leads; the design frames her food and never competes with it. Real images carry the warmth.
2. **Homey, not corporate; curated, not cluttered.** Personality and warmth delivered with editorial restraint — generous space, clear rhythm, a few characterful touches rather than decoration everywhere.
3. **One clear path to ordering.** Every section gently funnels toward the custom-order inquiry. The primary CTA is always within reach.
4. **Specific to her, not to the category.** It should look like Mckenna's brand — avoid any pattern a stranger could guess from "bakery website." Warmth comes from accent + type + imagery, not a default beige background.
5. **Mobile-first, Instagram-native.** Most visitors arrive from IG on a phone. Design the phone experience first; desktop is the enhancement.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast (no light-gray-on-cream); placeholders included. Fully keyboard-navigable nav and order form with real, associated `<label>`s and visible focus states. Every animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative. Touch targets ≥ 44px for the mobile-first audience.
